# ADR-130: Clinical Evaluation Response -- Dr. Agnes Ju Chang Feedback

## Status: IN PROGRESS | Last Updated: 2026-03-25

## Context

On March 25, 2026, Dr. Agnes Ju Chang provided a detailed clinical evaluation of Mela. Her feedback identified 16 specific issues organized by clinical severity. This ADR documents each item, its impact, and the implementation plan.

Dr. Chang validated the architecture ("the foundation is strong") and specifically endorsed:
- Privacy design (AI runs on phone, photos never uploaded)
- Four-layer safety system (AI model, literature, clinical checklists, demographics)
- Risk scoring vs binary classification

---

## Tier 1: Trust and Safety (Implement Immediately)

### 1.1 No "Normal Skin" Category (CRITICAL)

**Dr. Chang:** "If someone takes a picture of a freckle, birthmark or their leg, Mela forces it into one of 7 categories. There is no 'normal, you are good' option."

**Current state:** The lesion gate (`spot-detector.ts`) detects healthy skin and routes to a green "Your skin looks healthy" UI via the `healthy_skin:` error prefix in `MelaPanel.svelte`. This WAS a critical bug from v0.1-v0.9.0 and was fixed.

**Action:** Verify the healthy skin gate works reliably across diverse inputs (normal skin, freckles, scars, clear photos of arms/legs with no lesion). Add test cases. If the gate is too aggressive (forces freckles into classification), tune the detection thresholds.

**Bounded context:** Lesion Detection

### 1.2 Low-Confidence Default to Dermatologist Referral (HIGH)

**Dr. Chang:** On amelanotic melanoma -- "when confidence is low, default to seeing derm regardless of score."

**Current state:** Consumer translation returns "Unable to classify" (yellow) when confidence < 0.40 but does NOT explicitly recommend seeing a dermatologist.

**Action:** When model confidence is below threshold (< 0.40 for any class), or when the lesion gate confidence is borderline, the result should say: "We couldn't classify this with enough confidence. **See a dermatologist to be safe.**" This addresses both amelanotic melanoma (no pigment = low contrast = low confidence) and poor photo quality.

**Bounded context:** Consumer Translation

### 1.3 Photo Capture Guidance (HIGH)

**Dr. Chang:** "Most people will mess up - you need guidance for lighting, 6 inches away, clean lens, etc."

**Current state:** No pre-capture instructions. The trust banner says "Take a photo" but gives no guidance.

**Action:** Add a brief guidance overlay before/during capture:
- Good lighting (natural light preferred, no shadows)
- 4-6 inches from the spot
- Clean your camera lens
- Center the spot in the frame
- One lesion per photo

**Bounded context:** Image Acquisition

### 1.4 Fix Referral Letter Signature (MEDIUM)

**Dr. Chang:** "The referral letter has a referring provider's signature on it even though this is AI generated."

**Action:** Remove provider signature. Add clear disclaimer: "This referral was generated by Mela AI screening software. It is not a medical referral from a licensed provider. The patient is self-referring based on AI screening results."

**Bounded context:** Consumer Translation

### 1.5 Clinical History Questions (HIGH)

**Dr. Chang:** "We need to ask questions. Is this new? Do you have symptoms? Family history? Previously biopsied?"

**Current state:** We collect age, sex, body location. No clinical history.

**Action:** Add expandable "Clinical Context" section with:
- "Is this spot new or has it been there a while?" (new / months / years / unsure)
- "Has it changed recently?" (yes / no / unsure)
- "Has it been biopsied before?" (yes / no)
- "Family history of melanoma?" (yes / no / unsure)
- "Any symptoms?" (itching / bleeding / pain / none)

These feed into the Bayesian risk adjustment:
- New + changing = 1.5x melanoma multiplier
- Family history = 1.3x multiplier
- Previously biopsied = flag for recurrent nevi pattern
- Symptoms = 1.2x multiplier

**Bounded context:** Clinical Assessment (new sub-context: Clinical History)

## Tier 2: Accuracy and Equity (Next Phase)

### 2.1 SCC as Separate Category

**Dr. Chang:** "SCC is the second most common skin cancer. It can kill. It's currently linked to AK which is pre-cancerous."

**Impact:** Requires retraining with 8 classes. ISIC 2019 has SCC images that were mapped to akiec. Reversing this mapping and retraining would give SCC its own output.

**Bounded context:** Classification (model architecture change)

### 2.2 Phone Camera Validation

**Dr. Chang:** "All training came from dermoscopic images. A dermoscopic photo of melanoma is VERY different from an iPhone photo."

**Impact:** The single biggest real-world accuracy risk. Need to photograph lesions with iPhones and validate the model.

**Bounded context:** Feature Extraction (preprocessing for phone photos)

### 2.3 ONNX INT8 Independent Validation

**Dr. Chang:** "ONNX model isn't validated because it is compressed."

**Action:** Run the INT8 quantized model on the full ISIC 2019 test set (3,901 images) and compare sensitivity/specificity to the full-precision model.

**Bounded context:** Classification (validation, not code change)

### 2.4 Amelanotic Melanoma Warning

**Dr. Chang:** "Flesh colored melanoma that has NO pigment. Very difficult to see even by dermatologists."

**Action:** When segmentation fails (Otsu can't find a lesion boundary due to low contrast) AND the spot detector found something borderline, issue a specific warning: "This area has very low contrast. If you're concerned about a flesh-colored or pink bump, see a dermatologist -- some melanomas have no visible pigment."

**Bounded context:** Lesion Detection + Consumer Translation

### 2.5 Pediatric Disclaimer

**Dr. Chang:** "If a parent uses this for a congenital mole on their infant, this would produce inappropriate results."

**Action:** When age < 18, show disclaimer: "Mela was trained on adult skin lesion images. Results for children may be less accurate. Congenital moles in children should always be evaluated by a pediatric dermatologist."

**Bounded context:** Consumer Translation

### 2.6 Multiple Lesion Guidance

**Dr. Chang:** "If they take a picture of their arm with freckles, moles, scars -- which one is being analyzed?"

**Action:** Add pre-capture instruction: "Center one spot in the frame. Crop closely." Add image quality gate that warns if multiple distinct lesions are detected.

**Bounded context:** Image Acquisition

### 2.7 Fitzpatrick Equity Disclaimer

**Dr. Chang:** "If you have a drop of 30%, it is not a great tool. Reputational and equity issues."

**Current state:** Documented in README and quality scorecard. Not shown to users.

**Action:** When Fitzpatrick type IV-VI is indicated (via self-report or skin tone estimation), show: "Mela was primarily trained on lighter skin tones. Results may be less accurate for your skin type. We recommend consulting a dermatologist for any concerning spots."

**Bounded context:** Consumer Translation

## Tier 3: Longer Term

### 3.1 Rare Cancer Types (Merkel, DFSP, cutaneous lymphoma, Kaposi)
Requires specialized training data. Add disclaimer about 7-class limitation.

### 3.2 Acral/Subungual Melanoma (under nails)
Visually distinct from dermoscopic melanoma. Requires nail-image training data.

### 3.3 Tattoos, Scars, Piercings
Confuse segmentation. Add detection + warning.

### 3.4 Evolution Tracking (ABCDE "E")
Side-by-side photo comparison over time. Requires persistent storage + alignment.

### 3.5 PDF Summary Output
One-page printable summary for doctor visits.

## Consequences

### Positive
- Directly addresses every concern from a practicing physician
- Builds clinical trust through honest limitations disclosure
- Clinical history questions improve risk accuracy without model changes
- Photo guidance reduces garbage-in-garbage-out

### Negative
- More UI complexity (history questions, guidance overlays)
- Disclaimers may reduce user confidence (necessary tradeoff)
- SCC retraining requires compute time and validation
